## 🧩 The Business Context

IONOS is one of Europe's leading web hosting and cloud infrastructure providers. The Domains, Hosting, and SSL product division operates in a highly competitive European market — facing strategic pressure from both established players and agile challengers, while also navigating regulatory changes (eIDAS QWAC) and brand expansion into new markets (STRATO in Sweden).

In this environment, the division needed sharper, faster access to performance data and competitive intelligence to make confident product and prioritisation decisions. That's where this work began.

## 🛠️ Approach & Workstreams

### 📐 KPI Definition & Performance Dashboards

Collaborated with division management to define the right KPIs for tracking product performance across Domains, Hosting, and SSL lines. Built connected reporting dashboards in **Power BI**, integrating data from **Adobe Analytics**, **Elastic**, and **Looker Studio**. The dashboards provided management with a real-time view of priority issues — cutting decision lag on critical items by **30%**.

### 🔍 Competitive Intelligence — 15+ Market Players

Designed and delivered a structured competitive analysis across 15+ market players in the European web hosting and SSL space. Outputs were produced as concise **decision templates** — not slide decks — formatted directly for management consumption. This analysis directly shaped **Q1 2026 product priorities** for the division.

### 🌍 Market Research — STRATO Sweden Expansion & eIDAS QWAC

Conducted strategic market research for two distinct initiatives:
- **STRATO brand expansion into Sweden** — market sizing, user behaviour analysis, localisation considerations, and competitive positioning.
- **eIDAS QWAC compliance** — researched the regulatory landscape for Qualified Website Authentication Certificates, informing product positioning and compliance strategy for the SSL product line.

### 🤖 AI-Enabled Workflow Design

Evaluated available AI tools and designed AI-enabled workflows to automate reporting processes and improve analysis efficiency within the product team — a practical application of skills developed through Anthropic's Claude 101 and MCP certification track.

### 📢 Product Adoption & GTM Enablement

Supported product adoption by contributing to GTM assets, release notes, and stakeholder enablement materials. Also supported roadmap prioritisation, feature definition, and backlog refinement activities across the three product lines.

### ⚠️ Customer Escalation Management

Managed product-related customer escalations across product, UX, and engineering — each closed with a **documented, sustainable resolution** presented to leadership. Escalations were not just resolved; they were documented as decision records for future reference.

## 💡 Key Learnings

**On product vs. operations:** At Amazon, I functioned as an internal Product Manager without the title. At IONOS, I stepped into a product environment formally — and discovered how much of what I'd built operationally translated directly: stakeholder management, data storytelling, structured problem framing, and bias for resolution over diagnosis.

**On data infrastructure:** The difference between a dashboard and a decision tool is whether the right people can act on it. Defining KPIs before building the dashboard — rather than after — is the difference between a reporting exercise and an operational asset.

**On competitive analysis:** Effective competitive analysis is not about listing features. It's about identifying the strategic implications of feature gaps and surfacing them in a format that makes prioritisation obvious. Decision templates, not decks.

**On working in a second language:** Operating professionally in a German business context while completing a German MBA has significantly developed my ability to adapt communication style across cultural and linguistic contexts — a skill that is genuinely differentiating in European-facing roles.
